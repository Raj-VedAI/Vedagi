MycelAI Conductor: Flagship Deep-Tech Initiative

MycelAI Conductor: The Living Intelligence Platform

Peer-Reviewed Science Foundation: MycelAI Conductor is grounded in the landmark 2025 research by LaRocco et al. published in PLOS ONE, demonstrating that fungal mycelium networks exhibit measurable, signal-interpretable electrical activity. This peer-reviewed work validates our core thesis: mycelium is a natural, distributed computing substrate capable of sensing, transducing, and reporting environmental signals. LaRocco et al. (2025). "Signal interpretation in fungal mycelial networks." PLOS ONE. Foundation paper for MycelAI Conductor architecture.

We use the living underground root-like network of fungi — called mycelium. This fungal network naturally behaves like a biological memory device. It can remember electrical signals, store information without power, and survive harsh conditions where normal electronics would fail.

We created software that listens to the natural electrical waveforms this living network produces and turns them into usable intelligence.

So what does that actually mean in practice?

It gives us a triple-use platform:

1. Defence and ISR: Persistent Ground Sensors in Contested Environments

A military unit drops biodegradable MycelAI canisters into contested terrain via drone. Within 72 to 120 hours, the living fungal network colonises the soil and begins passively monitoring acoustic vibrations, seismic signatures, and chemical traces. No electronic emissions means no detection, no jamming.

When an operator sends a remote activation signal, the sensor nodes wake briefly, encrypt their collected waveform data, and transmit to the MycelAI Conductor platform. The operator receives a plain-language intelligence report: movement type, chemical signature, and confidence estimate. The network then returns to silent passive mode.

No batteries run out. No electronics fail. The sensors persist for months with zero logistics support.

EMP Resistant Radiation-Hard Zero Standby Power Drop-and-Forget Persistent ISR

2. Agriculture and Carbon MRV: Immediate Revenue for Alberta Farmers

A canola farmer in central Alberta inoculates a field with MycelAI substrate in spring. By midsummer, the living fungal network spans the entire root zone and continuously collects waveform data across soil moisture, temperature, and nutrient levels.

The MycelAI Conductor platform processes those waveforms and delivers plain-English farm advisories. Actionable guidance on irrigation and soil conditions helps farmers reduce water costs while improving yield. Carbon monitoring runs in parallel from the same network.

The platform automatically generates verified MRV reports for carbon credit markets. Farmers earn carbon offset revenue alongside their operational savings, with no additional sensors or field work required.

Soil Moisture Sensing Nutrient Monitoring Carbon MRV Plain-English Advisories

3. Energy Efficiency at the AI Edge: Solving the AI Electricity Crisis

A data centre facing rising AI compute demand deploys MycelAI bio-sensor networks around its cooling infrastructure and power distribution points. The living fungal network monitors thermal gradients, airflow patterns, and ambient humidity in real time, drawing near-zero power from the environment.

Waveform data streams continuously to the MycelAI Conductor platform, which identifies cooling anomalies hours before conventional sensors would trigger. Facility management systems respond automatically, preventing equipment failure and reducing overall power draw.

As AI energy demand grows, MycelAI shifts the sensing layer from power-hungry silicon to near-zero-power biology. The result is a smarter facility that consumes less and demands nothing from the grid to monitor itself.
